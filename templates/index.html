Run by graduate students and alumni from the Berkeley, Stanford, and Harvard–MIT Math Tournaments, ICMT is an independent nonprofit organization that aims to [spread mathematics and the spirit of mathematical competition to undergraduate students around the world].

Who's this for?

Division A

For the seasoned contestant

In addition to the Division B syllabus, topics include linear algebra, abstract algebra, real and complex analysis, as well as other undergraduate subjects.

Division B

For the novice contestant

Topics include those found in high school math competitions as well as single- and multi-variable calculus, differential equations, and basic linear algebra.

Those less familiar with math competitions are encouraged to participate in Division B!

Contest Format

ICMT consists of two team-based rounds and one individual round. Teams may have up to 5 students.

Power Round

A proof-based team round. Contestants will answer a series of questions centered around a topic, culminating in incredible results in the field of that chosen topic.

Individual Round

Contestants complete short-answer questions ranging over a broad reach of mathematical topics.

Constellation Round

With realtime submission and a live scoreboard, this round not only tests teams' mathematical knowledge, but also strategic planning and quick thinking.
